WHAT IS GIN? AND WHAT IS IT MADE FROM?


Gin is a distilled spirit that is made from grain
and flavored with botanicals.
It is most noted for its pine flavor due to its main ingredient, juniper berries.
There are a variety of styles, from the famous
London dry gins to modern gins
that are less piney and more approachable.
Gin is a Dutch creation and the English made it popular worldwide.
Today, it's made and enjoyed throughout the world; Spain, Belgium, the Netherlands, and the U.K. consume the most gin.
It's the main ingredient in a classic martini and vital to the iconic gin and tonic,
but there are many great cocktail recipes that show off the botanical spirit.

Gin was created by Dr. Franciscus Sylvus, a Dutch chemist, during the 16th century.
His original intention was to make an elixir that would cleanse the blood of those suffering from kidney disorders.
