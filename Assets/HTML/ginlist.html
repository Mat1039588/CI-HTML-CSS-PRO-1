TANQUERAY NO10


  • Country: Scotland
  • Distillery: Cameron Bridge, Scotland
  • Established: 1830
  • Owner/Distributors: Diageo
  • Style: London Dry Gin
  • ABV: 43.1%
  • Tasting Notes: perfumed, aromatic nose wth notes of grapefruit zest, creamy custard and clean juniper.
    A mix of incredibly clean, spicy juniper, hints of earl grey tea and cardamom. Very citrus-rich. Long, zesty finish.
  • Botanicals: juniper, coriander, angelica roots, liquorice, cahmomile flowers, orange blossom flavours.
  • Market Price: £26-30

Description:

Tanqueray no10 Gin was launched in 2000. It was named 10 because it was developed on the experimental still named 'Tiny 10'.
Tanqueray no10 features only eight botanicals. This gin may not have been possible in 1830 when Charles Tanqueray first developed
the recipe fir his gin. Fresh citrus, especially in the reliable qualities to make gin like this would have been quite expensive or just
simply not available in 19th century London on a predictable basis.
The whole citrus is distilled separately from the other botanicals. The two distillates are blended, diluted and bottled.

PLYMOUTH GIN


  • Country: England
  • Distillery: Plymouth, Devon, England
  • Established: 1793
  • Owner/Distributors: Pernod Ricard
  • Style: Plymouth Gin
  • ABV: 41.2%
  • Tasting Notes: full and peppery with juniper berry, lemon oil and dried herbs and a little fennel.
    Peppery and floral palate entry with hints of soft fruits, tangy juniper oil, pepper and coriander seeds.
    Long spicy finish with berry fruits.
  • Botanicals: juniper, coriander, orange peels, lemon peels, angelica roots, green cardamom, orris root.
  • Market Price: £25

Description


A long standing favourite topple, this gin was one of the few gins to have a geographic indication,
obviously pertaining to its eponymous city. However, this gin lost its indication in 2015.
This is still a unique product, crafted from several botanicals in pot still with rather short neck and is blended with
Dartmoor water. It is then bottled into the classic "shaving bottle", with its distinctive green glass.

HENDRICKS GIN


  • Country: Ayrshire, Scotland
  • Distillery: Girvan Grain Distillery
  • Established: 1963
  • Owner/Distributors: William Grant & Sons
  • Style: Scottish Gin - New Western Style Gin
  • ABV: 41.4%
  • Tasting Notes: hints of Pine, eucalyptus and lime, marmalade combined with juniper and spicy floral fragrances.
    Very light and fresh-smelling. Delicate Juniper leads to a savoury and lime cordial-led aroma with floral influence.
    Slightly sweet and silky smooth with burst of juniper , citrus with a subtle lingering finishing of cool refreshing cucumber and rose.
    Followed by cumin, cracked pepper and salty liquorice.
  • Botanicals: juniper berries, angelica roots, coriander seeds, orris root, cubeb berries, chamomile flowers, caraway seeds,
    elderflowers, meadowsweet, lemon peel and orange peel.
  • Market Price: £27

Description


This "most unusual gin" has been brilliantly marketed as a quintessentially British product,
associated with cucumber sandwiches and rose garden, whilst simultaneously being proudly Scottish in its origins.
Created in Scotland in 1999, Hendrick's was successfully launched in the US in 2000, followed by the UK a few years later in 2003.
It may be favoured with rose and cucumber, but it contains eleven more botanicals plus such pure water from
the Penwhapple reservoir, high in the hills behind the distillery. This water is so pure that it is not purified or treated,
merely filtered to remove sand. At the end of the distillation process, essence of Bulgarian Damascena rose petals and
Dutch cucumber are added. The rose petals are pressed to release the oil, which is then dissolved with alcohol
to produce the essence.
Cucumbers are smashed up in water and the flavours are extracted around zero degrees Celsius using a cold distillation process.
